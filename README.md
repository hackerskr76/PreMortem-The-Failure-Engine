# Failure-Engine
Think of your project as **the ultimate "What NOT to do" guide for innovators.**

Right now, the internet is flooded with success stories, but companies, scientists, and students constantly waste millions of dollars and years of time repeating the exact same mistakes because nobody talks about their failures. You are building the platform that fixes that.

Here is the exact breakdown of the concept and what you are actually putting on the screen for the judges.


## The Big Idea (The "Why")

When starting a new project, instead of googling "How to build X," a user comes to your platform and asks, **"Why did people trying to build X fail?"**

Your platform unearths the hidden post-mortems, broken code, failed policies, and dead startups so the user can avoid those same traps. It turns failure into a valuable, searchable resource.


## What You Are Actually Making (The "What")

You are building a web application called **"PreMortem"** (or a similar name). To win the hackathon, your app needs two core user experiences:

### 1. The Search & Risk Predictor (The "Consumer" Side)

* **The Interface:** A clean, minimal landing page dominated by a single, powerful search bar.
* **The Action:** A user types in their new idea, for example: *"An AI-powered app that tracks wild animal migrations using drones."*
* **The Magic Output:** The app doesn't just show search results; it generates an **Instant Risk Assessment Report**.
* It pulls up a card saying: *"In 2023, Project SkyTrack failed because drone batteries freeze at high altitudes."*
* It gives a summary: *"3 similar projects failed. Main risks: Battery life (60%), Local Regulations (40%)."*



### 2. The Anonymous Post-Mortem Vault (The "Contributor" Side)

* **The Interface:** A simple, frictionless submission form.
* **The Action:** A researcher, founder, or student fills out a quick form about a project that went bust.
* **The Fields:** They input: *What were we trying to do? What actually happened? What was the fatal mistake? What did we learn?*
* **The Twist:** It allows anonymous submissions so people can share their mistakes without hurting their professional reputation.


## The App's Flow (How it works under the hood)

```
[ User Types Idea ] ──> [ LLM searches your database ] ──> [ Spits out a "Risk Report" ]
                              of past failures                   & lessons learned

```

### In short:

You are making a **Search Engine for Mistakes** that stops people from reinventing the broken wheel. It saves time, saves money, and makes the innovation process way smarter.
